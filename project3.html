Winning With Simplicity

As an assistant vehicle systems engineer at General Motors, I developed Python code to map option statements to electronic control units, consolidating information and reducing the total number of buildable electrical configurations (TBECs) through the “Winning With Simplicity” initiative at GM. This enabled vehicle systems engineers to access and interpret data efficiently. The project helped reduce the number of possible combinations from hundreds to 85 TBECs for the Cadillac Lyriq, allowing GM to streamline production saving time and production cost.
